# Elliott Wave Theory

Developed by Ralph Nelson Elliott, this theory states that long-term market patterns consist of five successive waves in the trend direction, followed by three corrective waves.

## Bull Market Structure

Impulse waves (1-2-3-4-5):
- **Wave 1**: Initial move up from bottom
- **Wave 2**: Retracement (never below wave 1 start)
- **Wave 3**: Strongest wave — never the shortest
- **Wave 4**: Correction (never overlaps wave 1 price territory)
- **Wave 5**: Final push, often declining volume

## Bear Market Structure

Same five-wave structure inverted — five waves down, three waves up for corrections.

## Corrective Patterns (A-B-C)

After each five-wave impulse, a three-wave correction follows:
- **Wave A**: First counter-trend move
- **Wave B**: Retracement of wave A (often traps traders)
- **Wave C**: Final corrective move

## Fibonacci Relationships

- Wave 2 retraces 50-61.8% of wave 1
- Wave 3 extends to 161.8% of wave 1
- Wave 4 retraces 38.2% of wave 3
- Wave 5 equals wave 1 or 61.8% of waves 1-3

## Trading Rules

1. **Trade Wave 3**: Enter at end of wave 2 with stop below wave 2 low
2. **Fade Wave 5**: Watch for RSI/MACD divergence and volume decline
3. **Never count waves in isolation** — always consider higher timeframe context

## Limitations

- Requires experience for accurate counting
- Subjective interpretation varies between analysts
- Not suitable for newly listed securities
- Cannot forecast unprecedented market events

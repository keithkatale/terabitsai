# Dow Theory

Dow Theory, derived from Charles H. Dow's Wall Street Journal editorials, is the foundation of modern technical analysis.

## Core Hypothesis

The stock market does not move randomly — it is guided by specific trends at three levels.

## Three Trend Types

### Primary Trend
- Duration: Less than a year to several years
- Direction: Bullish or bearish
- Use: Position trading alignment

### Secondary Trend
- Duration: Weeks to months
- Nature: Counter-trend correction within primary trend
- Retracement: Typically 33-66% of primary move
- Use: Swing trading entries in primary direction

### Minor Trend
- Duration: Days
- Nature: Day-to-day noise
- Use: Scalping only — not reliable for long-term decisions

## Basic Assumptions

1. Market price is determined by demand and supply forces
2. Prices move in trends for long periods
3. Reversal or shift in price trends may occur
4. Charts can predict changes in demand and supply
5. Price patterns tend to repeat themselves

## Trading Application

- Identify primary trend direction on daily/weekly charts
- Enter in primary direction after secondary correction completes
- Use support/resistance and volume to confirm trend changes
- Do not fight the primary trend without strong reversal evidence
